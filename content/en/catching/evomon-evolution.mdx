<Section icon={Target} title="Strategic Leveling Steps for Your Evomons">
Effective leveling in Evomon isn't just about grinding; it's about making smart choices to accelerate your team's power. Follow these steps to optimize your Evomon's growth and ensure your resources are well-spent.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Basic Catch-and-Battle Loop">
    Begin by understanding how wild encounters, catching mechanics, training, and turn-based battles function. This foundational knowledge allows you to assess an Evomon's potential before committing resources.
    <Callout type="warning">
    Do not spend valuable progression rewards before you are confident an Evomon will genuinely contribute to winning fights. Blind upgrades lead to wasted resources.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify and Develop a Core Carry Evomon">
    Select one battle-ready Evomon to serve as your primary damage dealer and main force for PvE progression and boss preparation. This focus allows for rapid power gains in a single unit.
    <Callout type="info">
    A focused core carry clears fights faster and provides a stable foundation around which the rest of your team can be built. Avoid leveling every new catch evenly in the early game.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize EXP Fruit Usage on Core Team Members">
    Allocate your Medium EXP Fruits primarily to your core carry and any coverage Evomons that address its weaknesses. These items provide significant boosts, so use them where they have the most impact.
    <Callout type="tip">
    EXP Fruits deliver the fastest leveling value when used on Evomons that are consistently part of your active battle team. Avoid using them on duplicates or untested rare forms until your main team is stable.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Engage in Purposeful Battle Farming">
    Utilize repeated battles to not only raise levels but also to test skills and confirm which Evomons are prepared for more challenging content like bosses, dungeons, and PvP.
    <Callout type="info">
    Training is most effective when each fight helps test a specific role: main damage, elemental coverage, survival, or skill timing. Avoid aimlessly grinding without evaluating your team's improvements.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Prioritize Evolution for Team Value">
    Direct your evolution efforts towards Evomons that gain a clear, beneficial role after evolving and fit seamlessly into your active lineup. Evolution should enhance your team's overall effectiveness.
    <Callout type="warning">
    Do not evolve solely for collection or novelty if it doesn't improve your team's coverage or battle performance. Practical value should be the primary driver for evolution.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Test Your Team in Challenging Content">
    Regularly challenge map bosses, co-op dungeon runs, and PvP battles. These difficult encounters are essential for identifying weak points in your team's coverage, underleveled members, and skills that might underperform in real combat scenarios.
    <Callout type="tip">
    Harder content reveals the true strengths and weaknesses of your team. Do not rely solely on easy wild encounters to judge your full team's readiness.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="EXP Fruit Types and Usage Guide">
EXP Fruits are valuable consumables that instantly grant experience to your Evomons. Knowing when and where to use each type can significantly speed up your leveling process.

| EXP Fruit Type | EXP Value (Estimate) | Best Use Case | Avoid Using On |
| :------------- | :------------------- | :------------ | :------------- |
| **Small EXP Fruit** | Low | New catches to reach base combat level, filling small gaps | High-level Evomons, core carries (inefficient) |
| **Medium EXP Fruit** | Moderate | Mid-level Evomons, core team members, after evolution | Evomons you don't plan to use long-term |
| **Large EXP Fruit** | High | Core carry, high-tier Evomons, preparing for boss fights | Low-level Evomons (overkill), collection-only Evomons |
| **Mega EXP Fruit** | Very High | Instantly maxing out a new late-game acquisition, PvP prep | Any Evomon not destined for your top battle team |

<Callout type="tip">
Always use EXP Fruits on Evomons that are part of your active battle lineup. Spreading them too thin across many Evomons will slow down your overall progression.
</Callout>

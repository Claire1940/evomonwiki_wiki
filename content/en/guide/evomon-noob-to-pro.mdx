<Section icon={Swords} title="Efficient Leveling & Strategic Evolution">
To truly go from **Evomon noob to pro**, efficient leveling and strategic evolution are paramount. It's not just about gaining levels; it's about making those levels count by investing in the right Evomons at the right time and understanding how evolution enhances your team's overall power and utility.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Basic Loop">
    Before anything else, understand how wild encounters, catching, training, and turn-based battles work. This fundamental knowledge allows you to make informed decisions about which Evomons deserve your investment.
    <Callout type="tip">

    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Your Core Carry">
    Choose one battle-ready Evomon that can serve as your primary damage source for PvE progression and boss preparation. This Evomon should receive the majority of your early EXP and resources.
    <Callout type="warning">
    Avoid leveling every new catch evenly in the early game. This dilutes your power and leaves you with a wide roster of mediocre Evomons rather than a strong core.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize EXP Fruits Strategically">
    Spend Medium EXP Fruits on your core carry and the Evomons that provide crucial elemental coverage against its weaknesses. These items provide rapid leveling and should be used where they offer the most significant impact.
    <Callout type="tip">
    EXP Fruits are most effective when used on Evomons that are consistently part of your active battle team. Save them for your strongest picks.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Farm Battles with Purpose">
    Engage in repeated battles not just for experience, but to raise levels, test new skills, and confirm which Evomons are genuinely ready for more challenging content like bosses, dungeons, and PvP.
    <Callout type="info">
    Training becomes more valuable when each fight serves a specific purpose: testing carry damage, assessing coverage, evaluating survival, or perfecting skill timing.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Evolve for Team Value">
    Prioritize evolving Evomons that gain a clear and significant role in your active lineup after training. Evolution should improve your team's progression, dungeon readiness, or PvP pressure.
    <Callout type="warning">
    Don't evolve solely for novelty. Ensure the evolution provides tangible benefits to your team's coverage and battle performance.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Test Your Team in Harder Content">
    Once your team is leveled and evolved, challenge map bosses, co-op dungeon runs, and PvP battles. These harder encounters are the best way to identify gaps in your team's coverage or underleveled picks.
    <Callout type="tip">
    Harder content exposes weaknesses that easy wild encounters won't. Use these challenges to refine your team.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Remember, a well-trained and strategically evolved team will outperform a collection of high-level but uncoordinated Evomons every time.
